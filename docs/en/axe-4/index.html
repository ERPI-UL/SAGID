Axis 4: Supporting Territories in the Evolution of Practices

This axis aims to support territories by making the chair a space for the exchange, dissemination, and provision of knowledge through the demonstration of tools/methods developed in the previous axes, by training, and by showcasing best practices in experimental territories.

This research axis can itself be broken down into different activities:

Demonstrating sustainable roadside management by validating the proper functioning of the developed tools and serving as a showcase for best practices for territories and the general public. Training local stakeholders to highlight the benefits of systemic approaches in decision-making processes. Dissemination, communication, and exploitation of demonstration results with the goal of encouraging a shift toward sustainable practices.

The current progress and results of this axis are as follows:

Brunelle Marche, Mauricio Camargo, Christophe Bachmann (2024). Recovery of roadside biomass: the contribution of demonstrators to the deployment of a sustainable supply chain.

Brunelle Marche, Brice Corrigeux, Johan Artunduaga, Mauricio Camargo (2023). An Observatory of Roadside Maintenance Practices in France.
